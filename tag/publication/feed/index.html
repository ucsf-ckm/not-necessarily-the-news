Endnote for Systematic Review

Tuesday, November 8, 10 am – 12 pm
Mission Bay Library, Room CC151

REGISTER >

This “EndNote for Systematic Review” workshop is the third session of a four-part series designed for those considering creating a systematic review.

Learning Objectives: By the end of this session, participants will be able to:

  • import citations from PubMed, EMBASE, Web of Science, CINAHL and databases of your interest
  • find and remove the duplicates
  • locate full-text articles automatically and add full-text articles manually
  • annotate pdf documents
  • export the entire Endnote library to Excel

Prepare for Publication

Tuesday, November 15, 10 – 11 am
Mission Bay Library, Room CC151

REGISTER >

This “Prepare for Publication” workshop is the fourth session of a four-part series designed for those considering creating a systematic review.

Learning Objectives: By the end of this session, participants will be able to:

  • write a systematic review protocol and register it with the PROSPERO
  • review studies from title/abstract to full-text quality selection
  • write detailed search method section
  • follow PRISMA guideline to report your review process
  • compile comprehensive search strategies as an appendix

UCSF scholars can publish open access articles at no cost in Royal Society of Chemistry (RSC) journals through RSC’s Gold for Gold program. RSC launched this program in 2013 to help researchers who want to publish their articles open access (OA), but lack funds to pay the OA article processing charge (APC). The Gold for Gold initiative provides all RSC Gold subscribers with voucher codes to make papers available via OA, free of charge. The UC Libraries subscribe to RSC’s Gold package, thanks to negotiations by the California Digital Library.

The UCSF Library has a set number of RSC voucher codes and will assign them on a first-come, first-served basis. Voucher codes can be used to publish a communication, full paper, review, or technical note in an RSC journal under the publisher’s Gold OA option. Authors provide the code upon acceptance of their article in an RSC journal. For details, see “What is the process for authors under this initiative?” on this FAQ.

To request a voucher code, send an email to the Library, including the name of the author(s), journal title, and article title. The Gold for Gold program is available through 2015 for the University of California.

Curious about OA publication discounts with other publishers, or about the UCSF Open Access Publication Fund? See the Publishing and Open Access website.
