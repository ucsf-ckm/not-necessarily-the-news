Knitting to 3D Printing

April 6, 2016 By UCSF Library

Makers Lab LibGuide >

Back in late 2014 the Library formed a committee of select Library staff to explore the makerspace phenomenon that has taken root in libraries across the globe. What is a makerspace? The following video gets to the essence of the idea:

Makerspaces are essentially creative, DIY spaces where people can gather to make, invent, and learn. They often have 3D printers, software, electronics, craft and hardware supplies and tools, and much more.

After weeks of research and discussion the Library makerspace committee came to three key insights:

  1. Creative spaces can be a resource for stress reduction
  2. Creative activity can harness brain plasticity
  3. Creativity cultivate scientific innovation
  4. Makerspaces are fun

In 2015 the Library hosted a series of pop-up makerspaceÂ events to test our research and insights. With the success of our pop-up events, it became clear that a permanent makerspace was the next step. As of Monday, April 4, the Parnassus Library makerspace – the Makers Lab – is now open to UCSF students, faculty and staff. In the space you will find a 3d scanner, two 3d printers, raspberry pi’s, arts and craft supplies, play-do, legos and more. The Makers Lab is an ongoing work-in-progress and we welcome your suggestions for what to add to the space.
